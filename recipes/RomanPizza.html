Roman-Style Pizza

Description

This Roman-style pizza has a long-fermented thicker crust, and is absolutely awesome. Topped with prepared sauce, sausage, sweet peppers, and three cheeses, or with whatever you like, you'll want to make this on repeat.

Ingridients

Dough
  • 4 ¼ cups bread flour (500 grams)
  • 1 rounded teaspoon dry yeast (3.5 grams)
  • 1 ½ cups plus 3 tablespoons water (400 ml)
  • generous 1 ½ tablespoons olive oil (20 ml), plus 2 teaspoons more to oil container
  • rounded 1 ½ teaspoons sea salt or kosher salt (10 grams)
Pizza
  • 6 ounces Italian sausage
  • 1 cup diced sweet peppers and onions
  • 1 cup prepared pizza sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup shredded provolone cheese
  • 1 cup shredded parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon dried basil
  • 1 tablespoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, yeast, and water. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  2. Add the olive oil and salt, and knead the dough for about 10 minutes until smooth.
  3. Place the dough in a lightly oiled container, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 12-24 hours.
  4. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C).
  5. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
  6. Transfer the rolled-out dough to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Spread the pizza sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  8. Top with sausage, sweet peppers, onions, and cheeses.
  9. Sprinkle with oregano, basil, red pepper flakes, garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, and salt.
  10. Drizzle with olive oil.
  11. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly.
